Overview

I was a Software Development Engineering Intern at AeroTec, based in Seattle, Washington. My role as an intern was to assist with the onboarding integration of agile sprint planning practices. I worked with the development team to work on flight testing software used for real-time flight telemetry and data acquisition in the FAA certification process. During my time, I was involved in a team environment to effectively communicate and work together with engineering and IT teams to meet demands and needs. This would include feature implementation, and maintenance of software for employees to use.

I worked with VSTS and Mercurial for version control, Visual Studio to code in HTML/CSS, Visual Basic and XAML/Wpf to implement feature requests and modifications for flight testing software.

Company Service and Projects

AeroTec designs and builds propriety software for its engineers to use a comprehensive set of customizable tools for flight test, data management, and analysis. Software engineers develop their own solutions for each individual project's needs.

My internship experience involved feature implementation and maintenance of the software tools RAD, TOM, DCT and TIM.

  • RAD:A tool designed to allow the use of charts, gauges and graphs to collect real-time flight telemetry data in an efficient fashion.
  • TOM: A flight planning module that categorizes and logically organizes test conditions to allow efficient flight sequencing. It allows for automatic generation of test information that can be efficiently managed.
  • DCT:DataCenter Tools is used for analytics and data retrieval from flight tests. Used to assist in coming up with conclusive results and verify requirements to expectations.
  • TIM:A tool used in conjunction with TOM to add information to a database to give it a more manageable way of handling instrumentation (Has yet to be built).

Due to company policy and their ownership of all work I have done. I am unavailable to showcase some of the feature implementation and work I have done.

Daily Role and Work

The Software Development Team was transitioning from a Waterfall style of workflow to Agile Sprint Planning Practices. Part of my role was to assist in the onboarding integration of such practices while working on flight testing software.

The team transition from using Mercurial version control to Github and VSTS services. This enabled better clarity for maintainance, code review and feedback of tasks for a project. In addition, I participated in weekly scrum meetings to perform requirements analysis, feature prioritization, pre-planned scheduling of tasks, testing, user validation and feedback. This would help the software team gauge and organize tasks into manageable weekly chunks, and enable better communication amongst team members to jointly work on various features within the same project.

Software, IT and Dev-Ops teams gathered every other day to discuss goals, plans and ongoing work for each individual member.

During my implementation of features for software, I was to research and learn a variety of concepts such as user interfacing practices, HTML/CSS features, M-V-VM Model. I have taken notes and done enough research to perform my tasks efficiently and to the best of my ability.

In addition I had documened weekly progress reports for the work I completed, and completed goal setting exercises that were evaluated by my mentors to gauge my learning and experience.
