Prompt

Using Extended Reality Technology, create an application/simulation to help students better understand the Physics concept of Projectile Motion.

Virtual Reality applications are used for entertainment purposes, but the goal of this project was to use technology to improve education for students to better engage and be more involved with their learning.

Process

UBC AGDA Club (AMS Game Development Association) is a student run video game development club at the University of British Columbia. Every year, there are workshops, guest speakers, and projects to focus on educating members of technical tools used in game development. Upon joining this club, I joined the AR/VR division and worked with group members on a specific project to learn more about Unity and the various tools that are used in extended reality development.

Working with the Emerging Media Lab at UBC, teams were able to utilize the resources and space to collaborate, experiment and discuss.

  • Weekly scrum MeetingsUsing Slack, project members connect virtually to discuss project goals, deadlines and meetings. We were able to coordinate with the AGDA club as long as the AR/VR division.
  • Meeting with ClientMeeting with our client who wanted to utilize our project occured every other week or as needed. Early in the project, we would discuss project goals, project specifications, and learn about basic physics projectile motion. Eventually, we would present our work progress and continuously update a classroom agenda to teach using our project.
  • Work with Emerging Media Lab:Project tools such as HTC Vive Headsets and Updated versions of Unity were in the Lab. We would utilize this space to meet, use the resources and test our project features.
  • Showcase and PresentationAt the end of the year, the team would present the project as well as the teaching agenda to showcase to students. We would go to the school and showcase our project in a learning environment. Also AGDA would have an end of year project showcase where we would give a presentation of our work in front of the club and allow other members to try it out!

Due to company policy and their ownership of all work I have done. I am unavailable to showcase some of the feature implementation and work I have done.

Daily Role and Work

The Software Development Team was transitioning from a Waterfall style of workflow to Agile Sprint Planning Practices. Part of my role was to assist in the onboarding integration of such practices while working on flight testing software.

The team transition from using Mercurial version control to Github and VSTS services. This enabled better clarity for maintainance, code review and feedback of tasks for a project. In addition, I participated in weekly scrum meetings to perform requirements analysis, feature prioritization, pre-planned scheduling of tasks, testing, user validation and feedback. This would help the software team gauge and organize tasks into manageable weekly chunks, and enable better communication amongst team members to jointly work on various features within the same project.

Software, IT and Dev-Ops teams gathered every other day to discuss goals, plans and ongoing work for each individual member.

During my implementation of features for software, I was to research and learn a variety of concepts such as user interfacing practices, HTML/CSS features, M-V-VM Model. I have taken notes and done enough research to perform my tasks efficiently and to the best of my ability.

In addition I had documened weekly progress reports for the work I completed, and completed goal setting exercises that were evaluated by my mentors to gauge my learning and experience.
